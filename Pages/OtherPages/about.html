My Development journey

Primary

I had a small interest in electronic circuits but had only done a little bit of block coding using Scratch.

Secondary

2021: I start Secondary School (Year 7 and 8)

This was the first time i was introduced to programming fully, by this time I was not using Scratch but had forgotten most of it. Due to my special neededs I was placed in a special needs class for creatives, due to this I did very little coding and when we did it was using tykiner block coding.

2022: I start my GCSEs (Year 9)

My school started GCSEs in year 9. My school made everyone do a langauge, and history or Geography which left me with two options to freely pick from. I was certain on doing History and Geography which left with one choice, seeing as I had my interest in electronic circuits I choose to do Computer Science as my other option. Once the Computer Science course has started I was a fair bit behind the rest of the class but I did replit's course on an introduction to programming with Python, this helped me to catch up with everyone else, by Christmas I was one of the best in the class. I did not end up completeing the replit course but in lessons we were doing Python work anyway so once I had caught up, I was able to do the challenges set by my teachers well.

2022-2023: I do my GCSEs (Year 9 to year 10)

We carried on to do Python work all the way through year 10.

2024: Final year of GCSEs (Year 10 and 11)

Towards the end of year 10 and the start of year 11, is when we started the theory side of Computer Science. We still did regular coding alongside the theory.

2025-Now: Sixth Form

By this point I was actively enjoying programming, so I decided to take Computer Science as an A-level. I didn't really know what other subjects to do alongisde, I decided to do BETC IT as it is related to using computers.

My approach

Self-Teaching

I have often taught myself elements of computer science and programming, wether to catch up with my peers to develop my own skills outside of the classroom. For example, I changed my operating system from Windows to Linux.

AI as a tool

When developing programs, I use AI in a similar way to peer discussion: to question my thinking, not replace it.
